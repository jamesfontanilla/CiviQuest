# Verb Tenses

## Introduction

**Verb tenses** indicate WHEN an action takes place — in the past, present, or future — and whether that action is complete, ongoing, or habitual. English has 12 main tenses, and mastering them is critical for the Philippine Civil Service Examination (CSE) at both Professional and Sub-Professional levels.

### Why Verb Tenses Matter

Verb tenses are the backbone of clear communication. Using the wrong tense can:
- Change the meaning of a sentence entirely ("He **worked** here" vs. "He **works** here")
- Create confusion about when events happened
- Signal poor grammar competence in professional writing

### Why Verb Tenses Are Tested in the CSE

The Grammar and Correct Usage section of the CSE consistently tests verb tenses because:
- Government communication requires precision about timelines and deadlines
- Official memoranda, reports, and correspondence demand correct tense usage
- Tense errors are among the most common grammar mistakes Filipino professionals make

### Common Mistakes Examinees Make

1. Confusing simple past with present perfect ("I already **submitted**" vs. "I have already **submitted**")
2. Using wrong auxiliary verbs ("She **have** gone" instead of "She **has** gone")
3. Shifting tenses mid-sentence or mid-paragraph
4. Misusing the continuous form ("I am **knowing**" instead of "I **know**")
5. Incorrect irregular verb forms ("He **catched**" instead of "He **caught**")

After this lesson, you should be able to:

## Core Concept Explanations


### 4.1 Overview of Verb Tenses

English verb tenses are organized around THREE time frames and FOUR aspects:

| Time Frame | Simple | Continuous | Perfect | Perfect Continuous |
|-----------|--------|------------|---------|-------------------|
| **Present** | write/writes | am/is/are writing | has/have written | has/have been writing |
| **Past** | wrote | was/were writing | had written | had been writing |
| **Future** | will write | will be writing | will have written | will have been writing |

**Time relationship** tells us WHEN the action occurs:
- **Present** — now, habitually, or as a general truth
- **Past** — before now, completed or ongoing in a past period
- **Future** — after now, planned or predicted

**Action completion** tells us WHETHER the action is finished:
- **Simple** — states a fact or completed action
- **Perfect** — emphasizes completion before a reference point

**Continuity of action** tells us whether the action is ONGOING:
- **Continuous** — action in progress at a specific time
- **Perfect Continuous** — action ongoing for a duration up to a reference point

### 4.2 Simple Present Tense

#### Structure

| Subject | Verb Form | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | base form | I **work** every day. |
| He / She / It | base form + -s/-es | She **works** every day. |

**Negative:** do/does + not + base form
- I **do not (don't) work** on Sundays.
- She **does not (doesn't) work** on Sundays.

**Question:** Do/Does + subject + base form?
- **Do** you **work** here?
- **Does** she **work** here?

#### Spelling Rules for Third Person Singular (-s/-es)

| Verb Ending | Rule | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Most verbs | add -s | work → works, read → reads |
| -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -z | add -es | pass → passes, watch → watches |
| consonant + -y | change y to i, add -es | study → studies, carry → carries |
| vowel + -y | add -s | play → plays, enjoy → enjoys |
| -o | add -es | go → goes, do → does |

#### Usage

1. **Daily habits and routines**
   - The government employee **reports** to work at 8:00 AM.
   - They **submit** their weekly accomplishment reports every Friday.

2. **General truths and facts**
   - Water **boils** at 100 degrees Celsius.
   - The Philippines **consists** of over 7,000 islands.

3. **Permanent states**
   - She **works** at the Department of Education.
   - The office **occupies** the third floor.

4. **Scheduled events (timetables)**
   - The meeting **starts** at 9:00 AM tomorrow.
   - The exam **begins** on March 15.

#### Signal Words

always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/month/year, on Mondays, twice a week, generally, normally, frequently, seldom, habitually

#### CSE-Style Examples

**Easy:** The clerk ___ the documents before noon.
- (a) file (b) **files** (c) filing (d) filed
- *Explanation:* "The clerk" is singular third person; habitual action → simple present with -s.

**Medium:** Every government agency ___ an annual report to the Commission on Audit.
- (a) submit (b) **submits** (c) submitted (d) submitting
- *Explanation:* "Every government agency" is singular; "every" signals habitual action → simple present.

**Hard:** The committee, which meets quarterly, ___ its findings to the department secretary.
- (a) report (b) **reports** (c) reporting (d) reported
- *Explanation:* Subject is "committee" (singular collective noun); intervening clause doesn't change agreement; habitual action → simple present.

### 4.3 Present Continuous Tense

#### Structure

**am / is / are + verb-ing (present participle)**

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I | am + verb-ing | I **am writing** a memo. |
| He / She / It | is + verb-ing | She **is writing** a memo. |
| You / We / They | are + verb-ing | They **are writing** a memo. |

**Negative:** am/is/are + not + verb-ing
**Question:** Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?

#### Spelling Rules for -ing

| Verb Ending | Rule | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Most verbs | add -ing | work → working, read → reading |
| -e (silent) | drop -e, add -ing | write → writing, make → making |
| single vowel + consonant (stressed) | double consonant, add -ing | run → running, sit → sitting |
| -ie | change -ie to -y, add -ing | die → dying, lie → lying |
| -c | add -k before -ing | panic → panicking |

#### Usage

1. **Actions happening right now**
   - The supervisor **is reviewing** the applications at this moment.
   - I **am preparing** the quarterly report.

2. **Temporary actions**
   - She **is working** at the satellite office this week. (not her permanent post)
   - They **are using** a temporary ID while waiting for the permanent one.

3. **Future scheduled/planned actions**
   - The director **is meeting** with the staff tomorrow.
   - We **are conducting** the orientation next Monday.

4. **Trends and changing situations**
   - The number of applicants **is increasing** every year.
   - Government services **are improving** under the new administration.

#### Common Errors

**Non-action (stative) verbs** generally CANNOT be used in continuous form:
- ❌ I **am knowing** the answer.
- ✅ I **know** the answer.

Common stative verbs: know, believe, understand, want, need, prefer, love, hate, like, own, possess, belong, seem, appear, mean, consist, contain, deserve, realize

**Exception:** Some stative verbs have action meanings:
- I **am thinking** about the proposal. (= considering — action)
- I **think** it's correct. (= believe — state)

#### Signal Words

now, right now, at the moment, currently, at present, today, this week/month, look!, listen!

### 4.4 Present Perfect Tense

#### Structure

**has / have + past participle (V3)**

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I / You / We / They | have + V3 | I **have submitted** the report. |
| He / She / It | has + V3 | She **has submitted** the report. |

#### Usage

1. **Actions completed at an unspecified time before now**
   - The applicant **has passed** the civil service exam.
   - They **have reviewed** all the documents.

2. **Life experiences (ever/never)**
   - **Have** you ever **attended** a government seminar?
   - She **has never been** late to work.

3. **Actions that started in the past and continue to the present**
   - He **has worked** in the government for ten years. (still working)
   - They **have lived** in Manila since 2015.

4. **Recently completed actions (just, already, yet)**
   - The director **has just signed** the memorandum.
   - We **have already finished** the audit.
   - **Have** they **submitted** the requirements **yet**?

#### Since vs. For

| Word | Usage | Example |
|------|-------|---------|
| **since** | specific point in time | since 2020, since Monday, since January |
| **for** | duration of time | for five years, for two hours, for a long time |

- She **has been** a civil servant **since** 2018.
- She **has been** a civil servant **for** six years.

#### Already, Yet, Just, Ever, Never

| Word | Position | Usage |
|------|----------|-------|
| already | between have/has and V3 | completed sooner than expected |
| yet | end of sentence (questions/negatives) | expected but not done |
| just | between have/has and V3 | very recently completed |
| ever | between have/has and V3 (questions) | at any time in life |
| never | between have/has and V3 | at no time |

#### Signal Words

already, yet, just, ever, never, so far, up to now, until now, recently, lately, since, for, this week/month/year (when the period is not yet over)

#### CSE-Style Examples

**Easy:** The employee ___ all the required documents.
- (a) submit (b) submits (c) **has submitted** (d) submitting
- *Explanation:* Completed action with present relevance; no specific past time mentioned → present perfect.

**Medium:** The department ___ three new policies since the beginning of the year.
- (a) implemented (b) **has implemented** (c) implements (d) is implementing
- *Explanation:* "Since the beginning of the year" signals present perfect; the period is still ongoing.

**Hard:** The commission ___ yet to release the official results of the examination.
- (a) is (b) was (c) **has** (d) had
- *Explanation:* "Has yet to" is a present perfect construction meaning "has not yet done something."

### 4.5 Present Perfect Continuous Tense

#### Structure

**has/have + been + verb-ing**

- I **have been waiting** for two hours.
- She **has been working** here since 2019.

#### Usage

1. **Actions that started in the past and are still continuing (emphasis on duration)**
   - The committee **has been deliberating** for three hours.
   - He **has been serving** the public since he graduated.

2. **Recently stopped actions with visible results**
   - She looks tired. She **has been working** overtime.
   - The roads are wet. It **has been raining**.

#### Present Perfect vs. Present Perfect Continuous

| Present Perfect | Present Perfect Continuous |
|----------------|---------------------------|
| Emphasizes **completion/result** | Emphasizes **duration/process** |
| I **have read** the report. (finished) | I **have been reading** the report. (may not be finished) |
| She **has written** five memos. (quantity) | She **has been writing** memos all day. (duration) |

#### Signal Words

for, since, all day/morning/week, how long, lately, recently

### 4.6 Simple Past Tense

#### Structure

**Regular verbs:** base form + -ed
**Irregular verbs:** unique past forms (must be memorized)

| Type | Present | Past | Example |
|------|---------|------|---------|
| Regular | work | worked | She **worked** late yesterday. |
| Regular | submit | submitted | He **submitted** the form last week. |
| Irregular | go | went | They **went** to the seminar. |
| Irregular | write | wrote | She **wrote** the report. |

**Negative:** did + not + base form
- She **did not (didn't) attend** the meeting.

**Question:** Did + subject + base form?
- **Did** she **attend** the meeting?

#### Spelling Rules for Regular Verbs (-ed)

| Verb Ending | Rule | Example |
|-------------|------|---------|
| Most verbs | add -ed | work → worked, clean → cleaned |
| -e | add -d | file → filed, approve → approved |

- (a) find (b) **found** (c) have found (d) are finding
- *Explanation:* "When the auditors arrived" establishes a past time frame; the discovery happened at that past moment → simple past.

### 4.7 Past Continuous Tense

#### Structure

**was / were + verb-ing**

| Subject | Form | Example |
|---------|------|---------|
| I / He / She / It | was + verb-ing | I **was working** when he called. |
| You / We / They | were + verb-ing | They **were working** when he called. |

#### Usage

1. **Action in progress at a specific past time**
   - At 3:00 PM yesterday, she **was attending** a meeting.
   - This time last year, I **was preparing** for the CSE.

2. **Interrupted action (past continuous + simple past)**
   - I **was reviewing** the documents when the power **went** out.
   - She **was typing** the report when her supervisor **called**.

3. **Two simultaneous past actions**
   - While she **was filing** documents, he **was answering** phone calls.
   - The staff **were preparing** the venue while the director **was briefing** the speakers.

4. **Background/setting in narratives**
   - The sun **was shining** and the birds **were singing** when we arrived at the office.

#### Signal Words

while, when, as, at that time, at 3:00 PM yesterday, all day yesterday, this time last week

#### CSE-Style Examples

**Easy:** She ___ a report when the phone rang.
- (a) writes (b) wrote (c) **was writing** (d) has written
- *Explanation:* An ongoing past action interrupted by another action → past continuous for the longer action.

**Medium:** While the employees ___ for the meeting to start, the fire alarm went off.
- (a) wait (b) waited (c) **were waiting** (d) have waited
- *Explanation:* "While" + ongoing action interrupted by sudden event → past continuous.

### 4.8 Past Perfect Tense

#### Structure

**had + past participle (V3)**

- She **had finished** the report before the deadline.
- They **had already left** when I arrived.

#### Usage

1. **Action completed BEFORE another past action (the "earlier past")**
   - By the time the supervisor arrived, the staff **had already started** the meeting.
   - She realized she **had forgotten** her ID at home.

2. **Reported speech (backshift)**
   - He said he **had submitted** the requirements. (original: "I submitted...")

3. **Third conditional (unreal past)**
   - If she **had studied** harder, she would have passed the exam.

#### Key Concept: Two Past Events

When two events happened in the past, use:
- **Past perfect** for the EARLIER event
- **Simple past** for the LATER event

Timeline: had + V3 (first) ← simple past (second) ← NOW

- After she **had reviewed** the documents, she **signed** them.
- The meeting **had already ended** when he **arrived**.

#### Signal Words

before, after, by the time, already, just, never (before another past event), when, until, once, as soon as

#### CSE-Style Examples

**Easy:** By the time I arrived, the meeting ___ already ___.
- (a) has...started (b) **had...started** (c) is...starting (d) was...starting
- *Explanation:* The meeting started BEFORE "I arrived" (both past) → past perfect for the earlier event.

**Medium:** The employee realized that she ___ the wrong form.
- (a) fills (b) filled (c) **had filled** (d) has filled
- *Explanation:* Filling the wrong form happened BEFORE the realization → past perfect.

**Hard:** If the department ___ the budget earlier, the project would not have been delayed.
- (a) approves (b) approved (c) **had approved** (d) has approved
- *Explanation:* Third conditional (unreal past condition) → past perfect in the if-clause.

### 4.9 Past Perfect Continuous Tense

#### Structure

**had + been + verb-ing**

- She **had been working** for three hours before the break.
- They **had been waiting** since morning when the results were finally released.

#### Usage

1. **Duration of an action BEFORE another past event**
   - He **had been serving** the government for 20 years before he retired.
   - The employees **had been complaining** about the system for months before it was replaced.

2. **Cause of a past situation**
   - She was exhausted because she **had been working** overtime all week.
   - The ground was wet because it **had been raining**.

#### Signal Words

for, since, all day/morning, before, when, by the time, how long

### 4.10 Simple Future Tense

#### Structure

**will + base form** (predictions, spontaneous decisions, promises)
**am/is/are + going to + base form** (plans, intentions, evidence-based predictions)

| Form | Example |
|------|---------|
| will + base | She **will attend** the seminar. |
| going to + base | She **is going to attend** the seminar. |

**Negative:** will not (won't) + base form / am/is/are + not + going to + base form
**Question:** Will + subject + base form? / Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + base form?

#### Usage

1. **Predictions (will)**
   - The exam results **will be** released next month.
   - I think she **will pass** the examination.

2. **Spontaneous decisions (will)**
   - The phone is ringing. I**'ll answer** it.
   - I**'ll help** you with that report.

3. **Promises and offers (will)**
   - I **will submit** the report by Friday.
   - We **will not disclose** confidential information.

4. **Plans and intentions (going to)**
   - She **is going to take** the CSE next year.
   - The department **is going to implement** a new system.

5. **Evidence-based predictions (going to)**
   - Look at those clouds. It **is going to rain**.
   - The deadline is tomorrow and nothing is ready. We **are going to miss** it.

#### Signal Words

tomorrow, next week/month/year, in the future, soon, later, eventually, by next year, in 2025

#### CSE-Style Examples

**Easy:** The office ___ new employees next month.
- (a) hire (b) hires (c) hired (d) **will hire**
- *Explanation:* "Next month" indicates future time → simple future.

**Medium:** According to the memo, the training ___ on January 15.
- (a) **will begin** (b) began (c) begins (d) has begun
- *Explanation:* Future scheduled event announced in a memo → simple future with "will." (Note: "begins" with simple present is also acceptable for schedules, but "will begin" is the most standard choice here.)

### 4.11 Future Continuous Tense

#### Structure

**will + be + verb-ing**

- This time tomorrow, I **will be taking** the civil service exam.
- At 9:00 AM on Monday, the committee **will be reviewing** applications.

#### Usage

1. **Action in progress at a specific future time**
   - At this time next week, she **will be attending** the orientation.
   - By 3:00 PM, they **will be conducting** interviews.

2. **Planned/expected future actions (polite or matter-of-fact)**
   - The director **will be visiting** our office next Tuesday.
   - I **will be submitting** my application this afternoon.

#### Signal Words

this time tomorrow, at 3:00 PM tomorrow, at this time next week, all day tomorrow, when you arrive

---

### 4.12 Future Perfect Tense

#### Structure

**will + have + past participle (V3)**

- By December, she **will have completed** her probationary period.
- They **will have finished** the project by the deadline.

#### Usage

1. **Action completed BEFORE a specific future time**
   - By the end of the year, the agency **will have processed** 10,000 applications.
   - She **will have served** the government for 25 years by 2026.

2. **Assumption about what has already happened**
   - He **will have received** the memo by now.

#### Signal Words

by tomorrow, by next week, by the time, by then, before (+ future time), by the end of

#### CSE-Style Examples

**Medium:** By the time you arrive, we ___ the preparations.
- (a) finish (b) finished (c) **will have finished** (d) are finishing
- *Explanation:* Action completed before a future reference point → future perfect.

**Hard:** By next March, the department ___ the new policy for exactly one year.
- (a) implements (b) implemented (c) will implement (d) **will have implemented**
- *Explanation:* Completion by a specific future date → future perfect.

---

### 4.13 Future Perfect Continuous Tense

#### Structure

**will + have + been + verb-ing**

- By June, she **will have been working** here for five years.
- By the time the project ends, they **will have been developing** the system for two years.

#### Usage

1. **Duration of an action up to a specific future point**
   - By next month, I **will have been studying** for the CSE for six months.
   - By retirement, he **will have been serving** the government for 30 years.

#### Signal Words

by (+ future time) + for (+ duration), by the time

### 4.14 Tense Consistency

#### Rule

Maintain the same tense throughout a sentence or paragraph unless there is a logical reason to shift (e.g., reporting a past event's future consequence).

#### Common Errors

❌ **Inconsistent:** The supervisor **reviewed** the report and **approves** it immediately.
✅ **Consistent:** The supervisor **reviewed** the report and **approved** it immediately.

❌ **Inconsistent:** She **is working** on the project yesterday.
✅ **Consistent:** She **was working** on the project yesterday.

❌ **Inconsistent:** The memo **stated** that the meeting **starts** at 9:00 AM.
✅ **Consistent:** The memo **stated** that the meeting **started** at 9:00 AM. (reported speech backshift)

#### When Tense Shifts ARE Correct

- Stating a general truth within a past narrative:
  - The teacher **explained** that the earth **revolves** around the sun. (general truth stays present)
- Reporting current relevance of a past action:
  - She **studied** hard, and now she **is** confident about the exam.

### 4.15 Sequence of Tenses

#### Dependent Clauses

When the main clause is in the past, dependent clauses typically shift back:

| Main Clause (Past) | Dependent Clause |
|--------------------|--------------------|
| She said... | ...she **was** busy. (not "is") |
| He mentioned... | ...he **had finished**. (not "has finished") |
| They announced... | ...the office **would close** early. (not "will close") |

#### Reported Speech

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| "I **work** here." | He said he **worked** there. |
| "I **am working**." | He said he **was working**. |
| "I **worked** yesterday." | He said he **had worked** the day before. |
| "I **have finished**." | He said he **had finished**. |
| "I **will go**." | He said he **would go**. |

#### Conditional Sentences

| Type | If-Clause | Main Clause | Example |
|------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Zero (fact) | present simple | present simple | If you heat water, it **boils**. |
| First (real future) | present simple | will + base | If she **studies**, she **will pass**. |
| Second (unreal present) | past simple | would + base | If I **had** time, I **would help**. |
| Third (unreal past) | past perfect | would have + V3 | If he **had applied**, he **would have been** hired. |

### 4.16 Common Verb Tense Errors

#### Error 1: Tense Shifting

❌ The applicant **submits** the form and then **waited** for the results.
✅ The applicant **submitted** the form and then **waited** for the results.

#### Error 2: Wrong Auxiliary Verbs

❌ She **have** completed the training.
✅ She **has** completed the training.

❌ They **was** working on the project.
✅ They **were** working on the project.

#### Error 3: Misuse of Perfect Tenses

❌ I **have visited** Manila last year. (specific past time → use simple past)
✅ I **visited** Manila last year.

❌ She **worked** here since 2018. (action continuing to present → use present perfect)
✅ She **has worked** here since 2018.

#### Error 4: Incorrect Continuous Forms

❌ I **am understanding** the lesson now. (stative verb)
✅ I **understand** the lesson now.

❌ She **is having** a car. (possession = stative)
✅ She **has** a car.

#### Error 5: Wrong Irregular Verb Forms

❌ He **catched** the ball.
✅ He **caught** the ball.

❌ She **has went** home.
✅ She **has gone** home.

❌ They **have wrote** the letter.
✅ They **have written** the letter.

| Past Perfect | had + V3 | She **had written**. |
| Past Perfect Continuous | had been + V-ing | She **had been writing**. |
| Simple Future | will + V1 | She **will write**. |
| Future Continuous | will be + V-ing | She **will be writing**. |
| Future Perfect | will have + V3 | She **will have written**. |
| Future Perfect Continuous | will have been + V-ing | She **will have been writing**. |

## Worked Examples

### Example 1: Choosing Between Simple Past and Present Perfect

**Problem:** Choose the correct verb form.
"The agency ___ three major projects since January."

(a) completed (b) completes (c) **has completed** (d) completing

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. Identify the time signal: "since January" — indicates a period from a past point to NOW
2. "Since" requires present perfect tense
3. Subject "The agency" is singular → use "has"
4. **Answer: "has completed"**

### Example 2: Past Continuous vs. Simple Past

**Problem:** Choose the correct verb form.
"While the secretary ___ the minutes, the lights went out."

(a) types (b) typed (c) **was typing** (d) has typed

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. "While" signals a longer action in progress
2. "The lights went out" is the interrupting action (simple past)
3. The longer background action uses past continuous
4. Subject "the secretary" is singular → "was typing"
5. **Answer: "was typing"**

### Example 3: Future Perfect

**Problem:** Choose the correct verb form.
"By the end of this fiscal year, the department ___ over 5,000 applications."

(a) processes (b) processed (c) **will have processed** (d) is processing

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. "By the end of this fiscal year" = before a specific future point
2. Action completed before that future point → future perfect
3. Structure: will + have + past participle
4. **Answer: "will have processed"**

### Example 4: Past Perfect (Sequence of Events)

**Problem:** Choose the correct verb form.
"When the auditor arrived, the accountant ___ already ___ the discrepancy."

(a) has...discovered (b) **had...discovered** (c) is...discovering (d) was...discovering

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. Two past events: (1) accountant discovered discrepancy, (2) auditor arrived
2. The discovery happened FIRST (earlier past) → past perfect
3. The arrival happened SECOND (later past) → simple past (given)
4. **Answer: "had discovered"**

**Problem:** Choose the correct verb form.

(a) closes (b) will close (c) **would close** (d) closed

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. Main verb "announced" is past tense
2. Reported speech requires backshift: "will" → "would"
3. "The following Friday" confirms future-in-the-past
4. **Answer: "would close"**

(a) discusses (b) discussed (c) **has been discussing** (d) was discussing

**Step-by-step solution:**
1. "For three hours" signals duration

3. Ongoing action with duration up to the present → present perfect continuous

3. Structure: If + had + V3, would have + V3

## Exam Strategies

### Strategy 1: Identify Time Signals First

Before looking at the choices, find the time clue in the sentence:
- "yesterday," "last week," "in 2020" → Simple Past
- "since," "for," "already," "yet" → Present Perfect
- "now," "at the moment," "currently" → Present Continuous
- "by the time," "before (past event)" → Past Perfect
- "tomorrow," "next week," "will" → Future
- "by next year," "by the time (future)" → Future Perfect

### Strategy 2: Check the Sentence Structure

Look for structural clues:
- "While ___" or "When ___ happened" → Past Continuous for the longer action
- "If + had + V3" → Third Conditional (past perfect)
- "He said that..." → Reported speech (backshift tenses)
- "has/have + V3" → Present Perfect
- "had + V3" → Past Perfect

### Strategy 3: Elimination Technique

1. Eliminate choices that don't match the time frame
2. Eliminate choices with wrong subject-verb agreement
3. Eliminate choices with incorrect verb forms
4. Choose from remaining options based on tense rules

### Strategy 4: Watch for Tense Consistency

If the sentence has two clauses:
- Check if both should be in the same tense
- Check if one should be "earlier" (perfect) than the other
- Check for reported speech backshift

### Strategy 5: Stative Verb Check

If a continuous form (-ing) is offered as a choice, ask: "Is this verb stative?"
- If yes (know, believe, own, want, need, understand) → eliminate the continuous option

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Wrong | Correct | Rule |
|---------|-------|---------|------|
| Present perfect with specific past time | I **have seen** him yesterday. | I **saw** him yesterday. | Specific past time → simple past |
| Simple past with "since/for" (ongoing) | She **worked** here since 2018. | She **has worked** here since 2018. | Since/for + still true → present perfect |
| Stative verb in continuous | I **am knowing** the answer. | I **know** the answer. | Stative verbs don't take -ing |
| Wrong irregular form | He **has went** home. | He **has gone** home. | Memorize V3 forms |
| Tense shift in same sentence | She **opens** the door and **walked** in. | She **opened** the door and **walked** in. | Keep tense consistent |
| Wrong auxiliary | She **have** finished. | She **has** finished. | Third person singular → has |
| Past perfect without two events | I **had eaten** lunch. (alone) | I **ate** lunch. | Past perfect needs a reference point |

## Memory Aids

### Mnemonic: "SINCE = Starting point, FOR = Full duration"
- **Since** 2020 (a point in time)
- **For** four years (a length of time)

### Mnemonic: "HAD = the EARLIER past"
When you see two past events, the one that happened FIRST gets "had":
- She **had left** (first) before he **arrived** (second).

### Mnemonic: "WILL HAVE = done BEFORE future deadline"
- By Friday, I **will have finished**. (completed before Friday arrives)

### Tense Identification Hack: "The Auxiliary Tells All"

| If you see... | The tense is... |
|---------------|-----------------|
| am/is/are + -ing | Present Continuous |
| was/were + -ing | Past Continuous |
| has/have + V3 | Present Perfect |
| had + V3 | Past Perfect |
| will + base | Simple Future |
| will be + -ing | Future Continuous |
| will have + V3 | Future Perfect |
| No auxiliary (present) | Simple Present |
| No auxiliary (past form) | Simple Past |

### Recognition Trick: Signal Word → Tense

Memorize these pairings:
- **every day** → Simple Present
- **right now** → Present Continuous
- **since/for** (still true) → Present Perfect
- **yesterday/last** → Simple Past
- **while/when (interrupted)** → Past Continuous
- **by the time (past)** → Past Perfect
- **tomorrow/next** → Simple Future
- **by (future time)** → Future Perfect

## Quick Recap

| Tense | When to Use | Key Signal Words |
|-------|-------------|-----------------|
| Simple Present | habits, facts, schedules | always, every day, usually |
| Present Continuous | now, temporary, planned future | now, currently, at the moment |
| Present Perfect | unspecified past, since/for, experience | since, for, already, yet, ever |
| Present Perfect Continuous | duration up to now | for, since, all day, how long |
| Simple Past | completed past, specific time | yesterday, last week, ago, in 2020 |
| Past Continuous | interrupted/background past action | while, when, at that time |
| Past Perfect | earlier of two past events | before, after, by the time, already |
| Past Perfect Continuous | duration before a past event | for, since + past reference |
| Simple Future | predictions, plans, promises | tomorrow, next week, will |
| Future Continuous | in progress at future time | this time tomorrow, at 3 PM |
| Future Perfect | completed before future point | by next year, by the time |
| Future Perfect Continuous | duration up to future point | by + future time + for |

## Summary

Verb tenses form the structural backbone of English grammar and are tested extensively in the Philippine Civil Service Examination. The 12 tenses are built from combinations of three time frames (past, present, future) and four aspects (simple, continuous, perfect, perfect continuous). Each tense has a distinct structure, specific uses, and recognizable signal words.

The most effective CSE strategy is to identify the time signal FIRST, then match it to the correct tense structure. For questions involving two events, determine which happened first (past perfect) and which happened second (simple past). For reported speech, apply the backshift rule. For continuous forms, verify the verb is not stative. Master these patterns and you will handle verb tense questions efficiently and accurately on exam day.
